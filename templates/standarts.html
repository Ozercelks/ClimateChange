Carbon Emission Standards

Carbon emissions standards regulate the release of greenhouse gases from vehicles and industries. They aim to reduce air pollution and combat climate change by limiting the amount of carbon dioxide and other harmful gases emitted.

🚗 Automotive Carbon Emission Standards

  • 🌍 EU (2020–2024): 95 g CO₂/km for cars, 147 g CO₂/km for vans
  • 🔧 EU (2025–2029): Target lowered to 93.6 g CO₂/km for cars, 153.9 g CO₂/km for vans
  • ⚡ EU (2030–2034): Further cut to 49.5 g CO₂/km for cars, 90.6 g CO₂/km for vans
  • 🚫 EU (2035 onwards): Only zero-emission vehicles (e.g., electric, hydrogen) can be sold
  • 🇺🇸 USA (EPA 2032 target): Fleet average of 85 g CO₂/mile for light-duty vehicles
